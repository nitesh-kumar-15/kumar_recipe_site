Spicy Basil Fried Rice

When the craving for takeout hits on a busy Tuesday, this blazing‑fast, savory classic comes to the rescue. It is spicy, deeply aromatic, and ready faster than delivery.

Intro Ingredients Steps Tips

Act I · The Setup

This fried rice leans on day‑old jasmine rice, a hot pan, and a handful of aromatics. Once the wok is ripping hot, everything moves quickly, so have all of your ingredients measured and within reach before you start.

Act II · Ingredients

  • 2 cups day‑old jasmine rice
  • 1/2 lb chicken breast (sliced) or firm tofu (cubed)
  • 1 cup fresh Thai basil leaves
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 Thai chilies (sliced)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil

Act III · Steps

  1. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add garlic and chilies, cooking for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. Toss in the chicken or tofu and stir‑fry until fully cooked.
  3. Add the cold rice, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar. Toss vigorously to break up clumps and coat the rice evenly.
  4. Remove from heat, fold in the fresh basil leaves until slightly wilted, and serve immediately.

Act IV · Tips & shortcuts

Always use day‑old, chilled rice to prevent the dish from becoming mushy. Spread freshly cooked rice on a tray and chill it quickly if you don't have leftovers.
